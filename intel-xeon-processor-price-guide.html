The Intel Xeon families you will see

Xeon naming changed over the years, so here is the quick map of what is in the Indian market right now:

  • Xeon E5 v3 / v4 (Haswell/Broadwell, 2014-2016) — the budget workhorses. Hugely popular refurbished for value builds, file servers, and entry virtualization.
  • Xeon Scalable 1st & 2nd Gen (Skylake-SP / Cascade Lake) — Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum tiers. The mainstream choice for modern refurbished servers like the Dell R740 and HPE DL380 Gen10.
  • Xeon Scalable 3rd Gen (Ice Lake) — newer, pricier, more cores and PCIe 4.0.
  • Xeon Scalable 4th/5th Gen (Sapphire/Emerald Rapids) — latest, DDR5 platforms, premium pricing.

What actually drives the price?

  • Generation — newer Scalable gens cost more than E5 v3/v4 for the same core count.
  • Core count — more cores means a higher price; a 28-core Platinum costs far more than an 8-core Silver.
  • Clock speed & suffix — high-frequency "R", "M", or "Y" SKUs carry a premium.
  • New vs refurbished — refurbished Xeons typically run 50-80% below new pricing for the same chip.
  • Single vs dual pairs — for dual-socket servers you buy matched pairs, doubling CPU cost.

Should you buy new or refurbished Xeon?

For most Indian businesses, refurbished Xeon CPUs are the smart buy. A properly tested processor is effectively as reliable as new — CPUs rarely degrade — so you capture the same performance at a fraction of the cost. Pair the right Xeon with refurbished Dell PowerEdge servers or HPE ProLiant servers and you have enterprise compute at a fraction of new-build pricing.

If you want maximum cores-per-rupee, also compare against AMD EPYC servers, which often win on price-per-core for virtualization and HPC. For single-thread-heavy apps and certain VMware licensing setups, Xeon can still be the better pick.

How to pick the right Xeon for your workload

  • Small office / file server: Xeon E5 v4 or Silver — cheap, plenty for the job.
  • Virtualization host (10-30 VMs): Xeon Gold 6148/6248 — strong core count and memory bandwidth.
  • Databases / latency-sensitive: high-clock Gold like 6248R.
  • HPC / heavy parallel compute: Platinum 8280 or dual Gold.
